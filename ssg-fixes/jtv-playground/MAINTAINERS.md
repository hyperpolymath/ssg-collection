# Maintainers

This document lists the maintainers of the JTV Playground project, organized by the **Tri-Perimeter Contribution Framework (TPCF)**.

## Perimeter 1: Core Maintainers

Core maintainers have full repository access, deployment rights, and decision-making authority.

### Active Maintainers

| Name | GitHub | Role | Joined | Responsibilities |
|------|--------|------|--------|------------------|
| JTV | [@Hyperpolymath](https://github.com/Hyperpolymath) | Project Lead, Architect | 2025-11 | Overall direction, architecture, final decisions |

### Responsibilities

Core maintainers (Perimeter 1) are responsible for:

- ✅ **Strategic Direction**: Set project goals and roadmap
- ✅ **Architecture Decisions**: Approve major design changes
- ✅ **Security**: Review security-critical code, handle incidents
- ✅ **Releases**: Cut releases, manage versioning
- ✅ **Secrets Management**: Access to deployment keys, credentials
- ✅ **Community Governance**: Enforce Code of Conduct, resolve disputes
- ✅ **Mentorship**: Guide Perimeter 2/3 contributors
- ✅ **RSR Compliance**: Maintain Rhodium Standard Repository standards
- ✅ **TPCF Administration**: Manage contributor perimeter progression

### Decision-Making Process

- **Minor changes**: Any Perimeter 1 maintainer can approve
- **Major changes**: Require consensus from all active Perimeter 1 maintainers
- **Emergency**: Project Lead has tie-breaking vote
- **Community input**: Major decisions discussed in GitHub Discussions first

### Responsibilities

Perimeter 2 contributors:

- ✅ **Code Review**: Review and approve Perimeter 3 PRs (with oversight)
- ✅ **Issue Triage**: Label, prioritize, and manage issues
- ✅ **Documentation**: Maintain and improve documentation
- ✅ **Community Support**: Answer questions, help newcomers
- ✅ **Quality Assurance**: Test releases, report bugs
- ✅ **Mentorship**: Help Perimeter 3 contributors

### Progression to Perimeter 2

To become a Perimeter 2 contributor:

1. **Sustained Contributions**: 3-5 merged PRs demonstrating quality and understanding
2. **Community Engagement**: Active in discussions, helpful to others
3. **Code Quality**: Follows project standards, writes tests
4. **Trust**: Demonstrates good judgment and alignment with project values
5. **Nomination**: Nominated by Perimeter 1 maintainer
6. **Consensus**: Approved by majority of Perimeter 1 maintainers

### Progression to Perimeter 1

From Perimeter 2 to Perimeter 1:

1. **Deep Expertise**: Demonstrated deep knowledge of project
2. **Leadership**: Taken on significant responsibilities
3. **Volume**: 20+ merged PRs, sustained over 6+ months
4. **Trust**: Community confidence in decision-making
5. **Commitment**: Willing to take on maintenance burden
6. **Unanimous Approval**: All Perimeter 1 maintainers agree

## Maintainer Expectations

### Time Commitment

- **Perimeter 1**: ~5-10 hours/week for reviews, decisions, releases
- **Perimeter 2**: ~2-5 hours/week for reviews and triage
- **Perimeter 3**: No commitment required, contribute when able

### Code of Conduct

All maintainers must:
- Follow [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
- Model inclusive and respectful behavior
- Address CoC violations promptly and fairly
- Prioritize emotional safety and reversibility

### Stepping Down

Maintainers may step down:
- **Gracefully**: Give 2+ weeks notice if possible
- **Transfer**: Hand off responsibilities to others
- **Emeritus**: Move to emeritus status
- **Open Communication**: Explain reasons (optional)

Life happens. No shame in stepping down.

## Succession Planning

To ensure project continuity:

- **Bus Factor**: Aim for 3+ Perimeter 1 maintainers
- **Documentation**: All critical processes documented
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Regular handoff meetings
- **Gradual Transition**: Overlap period for new maintainers
- **Emergency Plan**: Designated acting lead if primary unavailable

## Governance

### Current Model

- **Benevolent Dictator**: Project Lead has final say (current: JTV)
- **Consensus-seeking**: Major decisions discussed and agreed upon
- **Community Input**: Open discussions for transparency
- **TPCF**: Three-perimeter graduated trust model

### Future Evolution

As the project grows, governance may evolve to:

- **Steering Committee**: Multiple Perimeter 1 maintainers sharing leadership
- **Working Groups**: Sub-teams for specific areas (security, docs, etc.)
- **Community Voting**: Formal votes on major decisions
- **Foundation**: Possible legal entity for stewardship
